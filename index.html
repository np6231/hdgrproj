The Glorious Revolution
Neil Parkhi

Article

The Glorious Revolution is something important which is part of what made our country. It taught people that a constitution is necessary just so the monarch/leader is not treating the people unfairly.
Back in late 1688, the English and Scottish Governments were absolutely angry at how King James II/VII (II of England and VII of Scotland) was not treating them nicely. So what happened is the government decided they needed a constitution. That became a serious problem for the government.
James had only one heir which was his son, and James got deposed in December in 1688 due to his unfair behavior. In 1689, William III of Orange was appointed to be the new King, and him and his wife Queen Mary II signed the English Bill of Rights, which was a new constitution for the English (later British) monarchy. After that William and Mary were co-monarchs (Mary II was a daughter of James II).

As William III was the King of England, he was also the Prince of Orange, making it complicated because he was the leader of two lands, which was also important because being a leader means you have to serve all of the countries you are a leader of.
In 1695, William III's wife and co-monarch Queen Mary II had died, so then William had even more duties to do, since then William III became a sole monarch for the rest of his life. He died in 1702, and then Mary II's sister became the Queen.
The Glorious Revolution back in 1688 was important, not only taught the English monarchy and gnernemtn not to let their leder be unfair to the government and people, it also resulted in teaching other countries as well, like it even got the United States to have that too, since it was during the colony period (While there weren't 13 full colonies) Now any country has regulation over their leader.

CLUSTERS

This is the war of the Glorious Revolution. It is serious because the government has been treated unfairly. Other countries also got offended because of this issue. This is how James II/VII got deposed.




The Glorious Revolution lead to the deposition of King James II of England (James VII of Scotland). James was treating government officials, citizen and foreign visitors unfairly. This is how it lead to the deposition.







After the deposition of James II, Mary II shared a co-monarchy with her husband William III of England (William II of Scotland). She signed the English Bill of Rights with William III to take on the monarchy a step forward and more fairly.







William III of England (II of Scotland) took on the English throne as he was appointed by the English Government to become king. His wife Mary II is where the future descent came from. William shared a co-monarchy with Mary II until her death in 1695, and continued as a sole monarch until his death in 1702, and then Queen Anne of Great Britain succeeded him.



The English Bill of Rights is an important document after James II/VII got deposed from all of the thrones of Great Britain. This document was signed by King William III and Queen Mary II so they could become both King and Queen of England, Scotland, and Ireland.
